Current Projects

Simon

Autonomous driving in the forest

Autonomous navigation systems are suboptimal in vegetated environments due to their inability to distinguish between traversable and untraversable vegetation. Through fusion of 2D and 3D perception and proprioceptive sensors, wheeled robots will effectively and efficiently navigate through heavily vegetated environments.

Proprioceptive Traversal

Robotic navigation where instead of treating all obstacles as untraversable, their cost are estimated for an initial path. Upon collision, the cost of the obstacle is updated and the path is recomputed.
